Deepening on HDDs

Historical Background

Since its debut by IBM in 1956, the HDD has been a fundamental component of data storage technology.

Over the years, it has evolved significantly in terms of storage capacity, size, speed, and reliability.

Only a few megabytes could be stored on the massive early HDDs, which were sometimes as big as refrigerators.

In addition to being small and portable, modern HDDs have the capacity to store several terabytes.

Benefits and Limitations

Benefits include:

  • Durability over numerous read/write cycles
  • Large storage capacity
  • Low cost per gigabyte

Limitations include:

  • Greater power consumption
  • Slower data access speeds than other internal storage types
  • Vulnerability to mechanical failures due to moving parts
