TLC and MLC Cells

NAND cells store information in the form of electrical charge. Each cell can contain multiple bits depending on the amount of charge present:

  • SLC Cells (Single Level Cell): store 1 bit per cell (0 or 1).
  • MLC Cells (Multi Level Cell): store 2 bits per cell (00, 01, 10, 11).
  • TLC Cells (Triple Level Cell): store 3 bits per cell (000, 001, 010, 011, 100, 101, 110, 111).
CHARACTERISTIC MLC (2 bits per cell) TLC (3 bits per cell)
Storage Capacity Lower compared to TLC Higher capacity with the same physical space
Speed Faster (read/write) Slower due to managing 3 bits
Endurance (write cycles) About 3,000-10,000 cycles 1,000-3,000 cycles
Cost More expensive Less expensive
Reliability Higher reliability and durability Lower long-term reliability
